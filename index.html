Let that number settle before you react.

If the total feels uncomfortable, that is not a sign something is wrong. It usually means these costs built up the way unspoken, invisible rules always do, not the stated way things are supposed to work, but the understood way things came to work, learned by observing, by listening, and by experiencing. The most significant costs are rarely about dramatic failures. They come from what became normal over time. You do not need to decide what to do next yet. Simply notice what stands out.
